About Me

I am an epidemiologist and associate professor at the University of Geneva with over 13 years of research experience in global health, HIV, and infectious disease modelling.

Research highlights:

  • Led analyses that improved understanding of HIV treatment outcomes in Africa and informed treatment guidelines.
  • Developed open-source disease progression models used for cost-effectiveness studies.
  • Co‑ordinated large COVID‑19 surveillance and modelling projects supporting Swiss public health authorities and the WHO Afro region.

Education

  • PhD in Epidemiology – University of Bern
  • Master in Statistics – University of Neuchâtel
  • Master in Biology – University of Basel

Current interests

  • Applying generative AI to automate data analysis and knowledge synthesis
  • Developing AI-driven decision‑support tools for public health

About the Course

Generative AI for Work & Research Productivity — Live Online Certification

This 8‑week hands‑on program teaches no‑code AI tools for automating data, content, and knowledge workflows. Participants design and build practical AI solutions to boost personal and team productivity.

Key outcomes

  • Automate document, spreadsheet, and data tasks
  • Create chatbots and RAG systems for project support
  • Generate professional visuals and presentations
  • Complete a real‑world capstone project

Course modules

  • Documents, Presentations & Websites
  • Automated Data Analysis
  • GenAI for Research & Text Analysis
  • Custom GPTs and Chatbots
  • AI Workflows for Task Automation
  • GenAI for Communication & Outreach
  • Capstone Project & Showcase
